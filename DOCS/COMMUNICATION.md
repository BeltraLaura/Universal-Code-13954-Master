## II. The Geometric Alignment Protocols
To ensure structural integrity and natural growth, all systems under UC 13954 must adhere to three specific geometric pillars:

### 1. The Phi-Scaling Standard (Growth)
* **Law:** Systems must scale using the Golden Ratio ($1.618$).
* **Verification:** $x_{n+1} / x_n \approx 1.618$.
* **Purpose:** Prevents "structural shock" during expansion by maintaining proportional integrity.

### 2. The Hexagonal Constraint (Economy)
* **Law:** Resource allocation and spatial organization should utilize hexagonal tessellation.
* **Verification:** Isoperimetric Quotient ($IQ$) $\approx 0.907$.
* **Purpose:** Achieves maximum volume with minimum material/energy waste.

### 3. The Recursive Branching Logic (Distribution)
* **Law:** Networks must follow sub-linear power law scaling.
* **Verification:** $Y = Y_0 M^\beta$ where $\beta \approx 0.75$.
* **Purpose:** Ensures equitable resource delivery from the source to the furthest endpoint without infrastructure bloat.

## III. The Entropy Shield & Sustainability
Systems are not static; they exist within time. UC 13954 manages decay through the **Negentropy Ratio ($J$)**.

* **The Negentropy Rule:** A system must export more disorder than it retains ($J < 1$).
* **The Jellyfish Clause:** Inspired by *Turritopsis dohrnii*, if a system reaches critical entropy ($J \geq 1$), it must trigger **Recursive Re-initialization**. It sheds 80% of its current complexity and reverts to its core "seed" logic to begin a new cycle.
